What is an MP3?

MP3 stands for MPEG-3. It is a digital audio encoding and decoding technology standard used for data compression of audio. MPEG-3 stands for MPEG-3 audio, which is a combination of MPEG-2 and MPEG-1. 


MPEG-2 is the compression, whereas MPEG-1 is the encoding. MPEG-3 is the hybrid of these two formats. It allows you to record music, create CDs, and listen to the audio on your computer or MP3 player. 


It is a lossy format, which means it will remove data from the audio file to reduce the file size. MP3 is one of the most common audio file formats in the world. 


It is one of the oldest formats as well, having been around since the 1990s. It is widely used because of its relatively small file size, making it easy to transfer music over the internet and on portable devices like iPods and smartphones.
